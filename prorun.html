ProRun

Mobile running app with an AI assistant to support users in achieving personal fitness goals.

Role: UX Designer
Context: Mobile app · B2C · AI-powered product
Scope: UX design · UI design · onboarding · monetization · marketing assets

The problem

Many running apps provide metrics and generic training plans, but struggle to truly guide users toward achieving a personal goal, while maintaining motivation and continuity over time.

The challenge for ProRun was to design an experience that could:

  • guide users step by step throughout their training journey
  • make the value of AI understandable and trustworthy
  • balance motivation, usefulness and monetization without compromising user trust

My contribution

In ProRun, I was responsible for:

  • Designing the end-to-end experience of the app
  • Defining onboarding flows and training plan setup
  • Designing the interaction with the AI assistant
  • Working on the paywall and CTAs, balancing UX and business goals
  • Ensuring consistency between the product and marketing assets for aligned communication

Screens & UX decisions

Activities: activity history and post-run feedback

  • Clear and readable activity history : Activities are organized to support quick access to key information, reducing post-workout cognitive load.
  • Qualitative post-run feedback : The AI assistant translates raw data into understandable and motivational insights.
  • Focus on progress, not just numbers : Each run is framed as part of a continuous journey.

Your Plan: training plan and continuity

  • Clear overview of the weekly plan : Immediate understanding of what to do today and what comes next.
  • Positive and motivational reinforcement : Completion messages sustain motivation over time.
  • Continuity across weeks : Reduced feeling of starting over.

AI Coach: explaining the value of artificial intelligence

  • Guided and reassuring conversation : Designed as a supportive dialogue.
  • Transparency behind training decisions : Explanations increase trust and adherence.
  • AI as support, not replacement : User control remains central.

Onboarding: reducing friction and misaligned expectations

  • Progressive disclosure : Gradual introduction of information.
  • Focus on personal goals : Built around user objectives, not features.
  • Clear product value : Differentiation communicated from the first screens.

Paywall and CTA: UX and business balance

  • Contextualized paywall : Introduced after value perception.
  • User-oriented benefits : Premium framed around goals.
  • Reduced decision friction : Clear CTAs and reassuring microcopy.

"You" section: gamification and motivation

  • Clear visualization of progress : Easy comparison over time.
  • Light and sustainable gamification : Motivation without intrusion.
  • Recognition of achievements : Emotional reinforcement.

Output

The result is an app that combines training, motivation and intelligent support, designed to accompany users over time rather than only during individual runs, fostering continuity and trust.

Results & learnings

  • Estimated increase in onboarding completion, driven by improved early clarity
  • Better understanding of AI value during initial usage sessions
  • Improved perceived balance between value and premium offering

This project allowed me to work on a B2C product where motivation, clarity and trust are central to the experience and directly connected to business goals.

Closing

ProRun represents an example of product-oriented UX, where design decisions align user behavior, emotional engagement and business objectives throughout the entire user lifecycle.
